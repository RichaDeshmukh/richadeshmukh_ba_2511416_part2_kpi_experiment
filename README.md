## Business Problem

The company launched a new onboarding campaign for subscription users.

Management must determine whether the new onboarding experience should replace the existing onboarding process.

The decision impacts product teams, marketing teams, customer success teams, and future users.

The primary goal is to improve paid conversion while maintaining user experience and revenue quality.

Risks include increased refunds, higher support tickets, slower conversions, and poor performance for specific user segments.

The recommendation should be based on statistical evidence from the experiment rather than observed differences alone.

## North Star Metric

### Selected North Star Metric

**Paid Conversion Rate**

### Why this is the North Star Metric

The primary objective of the onboarding experiment is to increase the number of users who become paying subscribers. Paid Conversion Rate directly measures whether the new onboarding experience successfully converts users into customers. Since subscription revenue is the company's main source of business growth, this metric best represents the success of the experiment.

### Supporting Metrics

The following metrics help explain user behavior but are not the primary success metric:

- Landing Page Visit Rate
- Trial Start Rate
- Onboarding Completion Rate
- Average Revenue Per User
- Engagement Score

These metrics explain different stages of the customer journey leading to paid conversion.

### Connection to Business Growth

A higher Paid Conversion Rate increases the number of paying subscribers, leading to higher recurring revenue and sustainable business growth.

### Risks of Optimizing Only This Metric

If the company focuses only on increasing Paid Conversion Rate, customer experience may decline. Users may request more refunds, generate more support tickets, or become less satisfied. Therefore, guardrail metrics such as Refund Rate, Support Ticket Rate, Engagement Score, and Days to Convert should also be monitored.
